I, Saleh, Mominur and Zubair on the day of Final Presentation

In Mechanical Engineering department we have to complete this sessional course ME 366 (Electro-Mechanical System Design and Practice). To complete this course students are divided into groups of 4. Each group is assigned a project. In our case it was a Trash Collecting bot project. So basically what we had to make was a robot - that can collect trash on its own, without human help. Sounds simple, right? Well, No! Let's break down the tasks. The robot has to see an object; detect whether it is a trash or not or what kind of trash it is; move towards the trash; grab it with it's robotic arm and place that trash in its container. So we had to do all kinds of engineering stuff (Servo motors, Arduino, Motor drivers, Bluetooth module, ESP32 CAM, LIPO battery, Ultrasonic sensor - we used these components). We had to 3D-print the arm of our robot.


Having past experience in programming with Python I got the responsibility for the Trash-Detection part. My job was to train an AI model to detect 3 Objects (Bottle, Paper and Can) and detect them using ESP32 CAM. I used YOLOv8, to train the model with photos of bottle, paper and can; and used a few funtions of OpenCV to detect the trash. I had to do a lot of learning and tinkering. And I did it! AND my friends also did their job as well and at the end we were successful in making such a robot that can detect trash and collect it. We did not give our 100% as it was just a 1.5 credit course. I think the credit for such an educative course should have been higher; And universities should add more such courses for students to learn the true essence of engineering. There is no financial loss in it for the university anyway. The students pay for the equipments from their own pocket. But the experience is worth every penny.
